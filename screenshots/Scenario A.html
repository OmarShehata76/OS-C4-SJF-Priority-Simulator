SJF Analysis

SJF always picks the process with the shortest remaining burst time. In this workload, SJF produced lower average waiting time. Short-burst processes gained a significant advantage.

Priority Analysis

Priority scheduling favors lower priority numbers. Starvation risk detected: some processes waited a long time due to being preempted by higher-priority jobs.

8

Final Conclusion

Conclusion & Recommendation

Based on the simulation results: SJF achieved better average waiting time, SJF achieved better average turnaround time, and SJF had better average response time.

Main Trade-off: SJF optimizes for throughput by always executing the shortest available job, which minimizes average waiting time but may delay long jobs indefinitely. Priority Scheduling ensures that important/urgent processes get CPU time quickly, but can cause starvation for low-priority processes as observed in this workload.

Fairness: SJF is fairer in terms of overall metrics but unfair to long processes. Priority is fairer to urgent tasks but can starve low-priority ones.

Recommendation for this workload: SJF — because it produced lower average waiting and turnaround times, making it more efficient for this workload.
